Planet label XR proof

The proof forces one Uranus marker and one finite world-anchored label. It is diagnostic presentation geometry, not astronomical distance.

Eye modes are reversible XR presentation controls for investigating binocular doubling. Assigning different layers to different eyes may cause binocular rivalry. Desktop remains a visible mono fallback for every mode.

World-scale WGS84 model: one Earth-centered structure contains the core, rotational spindle, projective NCP/SCP directions, and a finite reference ring in the celestial-equatorial plane. The optional 24 m local horizon is intentionally observer-centered and tangent-plane based; its radius is a visual reference, not distance to the natural horizon. The room floor is not an opaque Earth surface.

Sun and Moon remain separate categories. Mercury through Neptune are major planets; Pluto is a dwarf planet. Their markers are actual apparent topocentric directions at the selected observer and UTC instant. Planet Labels affects only planet/Pluto labels and defaults off; marker sizes are symbolic visibility aids, not planetary disks.

The optional Sun path is an observer-relative apparent trace across the selected local civil day. Its notches are independently calculated at valid civil-hour boundaries on the path itself, including below-horizon positions; it is neither the celestial equator nor an annual ecliptic.

The coordinate grid is a restrained geocentric reference: four closed declination circles and twelve intentionally open pole-to-pole longitude arcs. Its current 0h reference is deterministic and non-sidereal; future constellations must reuse this coordinate utility rather than introduce another convention.
